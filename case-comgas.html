Overview

A full homepage redesign for Comgás, the largest natural gas distributor in Brazil — transforming a dated, information-heavy page into a modern, conversion-focused experience that serves millions of residential and business customers.

comgas.com.br
01 — Challenge

Understanding the
problem space

Comgás's existing homepage had accumulated years of content without a clear information hierarchy. Users arrived with very different goals — paying bills, reporting issues, requesting new connections — and couldn't quickly find their path.

The page suffered from low conversion rates on key service CTAs, a fragmented visual identity, and poor performance on mobile devices, which had become the primary access point for most customers.

The challenge was to design a page that served a diverse audience — from households to large industrial clients — while radically simplifying navigation and establishing a cohesive brand presence.

Key Insight

"Users arrived knowing what they needed, but couldn't find it in under 10 seconds — leading to drop-offs and call center overload."

03 — Solution

A page built
for action

The redesigned homepage centers on a clear task-first hierarchy: a prominent search and "quick access" bar above the fold surfaces the top 5 user tasks (bill payment, service requests, outage reporting, and more) immediately.

We introduced a segmented navigation — residential vs. business — that dynamically adapts content to context, reducing cognitive load for each audience segment.

A modular component system was built in Figma with a design token structure aligned to Comgás's brand refresh, enabling the internal design team to maintain and evolve the system independently going forward.
